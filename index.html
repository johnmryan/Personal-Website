About

Jack Ryan

Student | Software Engineer

I am originally from Hinsdale, a small suburb west of Chicago. At Notre Dame, I currently serve as the president of the ACM Computer Club and I am an active member of the Bengal Bouts boxing club.

I generally spend my free time learning as much as I can about web development and exploring other areas of Computer Science. I am almost always enrolled in a course on Udemy, and I am currently enrolled in a Data Science course on Coursera. I have also become an avid Game of Thrones follower and can never seem to get over those frustrating season finales. When I am not sitting at my computer coding, I am often spending time with friends and family, playing basketball at the local rec center, or hiking.

My focus lies in Web and Android development, although I am always open to exploring new ideas. Any time I get to experiment with a new JavaScript framework, CSS animation, or any other situation where I can put my coding knowledge to the test, I am excited and ready for the challenge. My knowledge right now is somewhat limited to the front-end, although I will be taking a Database Concepts and a Data Structures course this semester, and hope to become a full-stack web developer soon. Above all else, I am eager to continue learning in order to make a substantial difference in this world of constant innovation.

Projects

  • Spring 2015

    Android PHR

    Worked alongside a graphic designer and a back-end team to develop the Android Personal Health Records app for Notre Dame. The application calls a REST service to send and retrieve measurable data, as well as to register and authenticate new users.

  • Spring 2015

    Tower Defense

    For our final project in Fundamentals of Computing II, my group members and I developed a Tower Defense game in C++, using the SDL/SDL2 graphics library. The program was tested with Valgrind semi-regularly to maintain efficient memory management. You can check out the code on my GitHub and play it as well via the instructions in the readme file.

  • Summer 2015

    Customer Support Tool

    As a Software Engineering Intern at TransUnion, I worked in a team to develop the new Customer Support Tool. I designed and developed the front-end via the ExtJS framework, as well as filtered and analyzed data from the back-end in Java.

  • Fall 2015

    Personal Portfolio

    During the first few weeks of my Junior year at Notre Dame, I developed this personal website using Twitter Bootstrap for responsive design, as well as Animate.css for some easy animations. This replaces my old website, created using the WordPress CMS.
